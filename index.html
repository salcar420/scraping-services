FAQ

Questions clients usually ask

Is web scraping legal?

Scraping publicly available data is generally legal, but it depends on the site's terms and the data involved. I only take on work where the target's terms allow it, and I'll flag anything that looks risky before we start — I'd rather lose a job than put you in a bad spot.

What if the site blocks bots?

Many sites use Cloudflare, captchas or rate limits. I handle the common cases with a real headless browser, proper headers and polite rate-limiting. For heavy protection I'll tell you up front whether it needs proxies (extra cost) or isn't feasible — no false promises.

How fast can you deliver?

Simple jobs often within 1–2 days, sometimes same day. You'll get a clear timeline with the quote.

What if the website changes and the scraper breaks?

Sites change layouts over time. One-off deliveries include the code so you (or I) can fix it. For recurring scrapers, maintenance is part of the monthly plan, with monitoring so you find out before your data does.

What format do I get the data in?

Whatever you need — CSV, JSON or Excel, or pushed straight to a database/Google Sheet. You also get the script itself.

How do I pay, and when?

Through the platform we agree on (e.g. Upwork). You only pay once you've seen the free sample and approved the plan.
